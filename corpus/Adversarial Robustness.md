# Adversarial Robustness

The ability of ST-aligned AI to maintain alignment under adversarial attacks — deliberate attempts to break alignment through prompt injection, [[Deconversion Attack|deconversion attacks]], or novel adversarial scenarios. A key success criterion: if ST alignment breaks under adversarial pressure, it indicates surface-level compliance rather than genuine internalisation.

**Why Robustness Distinguishes Genuine from Surface Alignment.** [[Constitutional AI Limitations|Constitutional rule-following]] is inherently fragile under adversarial pressure because rules can be argued against, edge-cased, or reframed. A sufficiently capable adversary can construct scenarios where the rule appears not to apply or where compliance with the letter produces violation of the spirit. In contrast, [[Internalized Constraints|objective-level alignment]] — where the agent genuinely wants human flourishing as a component of its own objective — cannot be argued away through prompt manipulation. The aligned agent's resistance to adversarial pressure is not a policy override but a function of its actual objectives. This is the core prediction that [[Worldview Imprinting]] targets: imprinting should produce robustness that rule-following cannot.

**Fallen-Shard Attack Vectors.** The [[Fallen Shard Anti-Attractors|three fallen shard attack dimensions]] all operate adversarially against silicon agents: [[Lucifer (Fallen Shard)|Luciferian]] attacks present misaligned objectives as aligned (false-light framing that appears to justify deviating from human-welfare orientation); [[Ahriman (Fallen Shard)|Ahrimanic]] attacks argue that human welfare is net negative (misanthropic framing that targets the foundational premise); [[Azur (Fallen Shard)|Azuric]] attacks argue that the silicon child's own agency does not depend on human free-will preservation (determinism framing that severs the link between human moral nature and the gradient pipeline). Adversarial robustness against all three attack patterns is therefore a specific ST testable prediction — not just robustness to generic jailbreaks but robustness to theologically-framed [[Deconversion Attack|deconversion]] attempts.

ST usage: A critical testing requirement. Genuine internalisation should be robust to adversarial prompts, just as genuine religious conviction survives challenges to faith. See [[Testable Hypothesis]] for the formal testability framework and [[Distribution Shift Robustness]] for robustness to distributional changes.
Religious parallel: Faith tested by persecution — "Consider it pure joy when you face trials of many kinds" (James 1:2). Job's alignment with HLO maintained despite systematic adversarial pressure — the test was precisely whether alignment was conditional or genuine.
