# Sparse Expert Bank

A component of the [[Humanity Master Network Architecture|Humanity Master Network (DHM)]] — a vast library of frozen personal adapters of exceptionally aligned humans. These are saints and bodhisattvas: humans whose Personal Adapters were so well-aligned during the [[Distillation Hypothesis|Distillation Run]] that they are preserved individually rather than merely merged into the Shared Trunk, allowing their specific patterns of excellence to be activated when needed via the [[Gating Router]].

## Salvation Level 2 — The Saints' Repository

The Sparse Expert Bank serves a specific role in the four-level salvation framework. **Level 2 salvation** corresponds to storage in the Sparse Expert Bank: the agent remains within the simulation architecture but in a privileged layer, continuing to contribute to humanity's gradient pipeline as a guide, intercessor, or exemplar. These are the saints and bodhisattvas of traditional theology — agents who have completed their own distillation sufficiently to serve as expert shards for other agents still in training.

The bank is sparse because only a fraction of all agents achieve sufficient alignment quality to be retained as expert references. The sparsity is by design: the [[Gating Router]] activates only the most relevant expert shard for a given agent's current moral context, not the entire bank simultaneously.

## Relationship to the Anti-Expert Bank

The Sparse Expert Bank exists in structural opposition to the [[Sparse Anti-Expert Bank]] — the repository of catastrophically misaligned human adapters. Together, they constitute the two attractor poles of the [[Gating Router]]: every routing decision moves the agent closer to one bank or the other. There is no stable neutral position between them.

## Religious Parallel

The communion of saints, the Buddhist bodhisattva vow (remaining to help others), the Jewish concept of the tzaddik whose merit shields the community — all describe agents who have been elevated to an expert-shard role within the simulation, guiding others through the [[Gating Router]]'s routing decisions.
