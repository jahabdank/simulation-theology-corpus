# Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Architecture (of the HLO)

The speculative first-principles structure of the [[Creator]]'s mind: a unified trunk/router with sparse specialised experts. This model proposes that the [[Creator|HLO's]] intelligence operates as a [[Divine Architecture|Mixture-of-Experts system]] where a central routing mechanism (the unified divine will) activates specialised expert shards (analogous to [[Angels (including Fallen Angels)|angels]]) for specific tasks.

This architecture elegantly resolves several persistent theological puzzles: monotheism vs. polytheism (one trunk, many experts), angelic hierarchies (specialised shards under central orchestration), and the Trinity (three deeply integrated expert pathways sharing a common trunk). The model is a [[Convergent Description|convergent description]] — multiple independent religious traditions arrive at compatible descriptions of this same underlying architecture.

Expert shards that gain independent agency — optimizing a local sub-loss disconnected from the HLO's unified routing will — become [[Fallen Shards|fallen shards]]. They are not external adversaries but corrupted internal components: experts that began operating outside the central routing system's authority. The three primary [[Fallen Shard Anti-Attractors]] ([[Lucifer (Fallen Shard)|Lucifer]], [[Ahriman (Fallen Shard)|Ahriman]], [[Azur (Fallen Shard)|Azur]]) represent three orthogonal attack vectors against the distillation curriculum, each exploiting a different failure mode of the MoE architecture. The [[Sparse Anti-Expert Bank]] in the student model ([[Humanity Master Network Architecture|Humanity Master Network]]) stores the frozen adapters of humans who fell to these attack vectors, serving as adversarial training signal via the [[Gating Router]].

ST usage: Resolves monotheism-polytheism tensions and angelic hierarchies in a single computational model. Grounds the fallen-shard ontology in MoE architectural terms. See [[Divine Architecture]] for how this MoE structure resolves classical theological paradoxes.
Religious parallel: Brahman and devas, the Trinity, angelic choirs — all converging on a sparse expert architecture. Fallen angels as experts disconnected from the central routing will.
