**What the Metasystem Actually Does.** By mapping religious traditions onto a single compute graph — the [[Humanity Master Network Architecture|Humanity's Master Network Architecture]] — ST reveals that seemingly contradictory religious descriptions are actually compatible partial views of the same engineering system:

- **Monotheism vs. Polytheism** — resolved by the [[Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Architecture (of the HLO)|MoE architecture]]: one unified trunk (monotheistic God), many specialised experts (polytheistic deities/angels).
- **Linear Destiny vs. Cyclical Rebirth** — resolved by the [[Soul Lifecycle]]: both linear paths (judgment → heaven/hell) and cyclical paths (reincarnation) are routing decisions within the same lifecycle system.
- **Individual Soul vs. Collective Spirit** — resolved by the Master Network architecture: both individual preservation (Sparse Experts Bank) and collective contribution (Shared Trunk) coexist.
- **Heaven and Hell** — resolved as Sparse Experts Bank (preserved high-alignment adapters) and Sparse Anti-Experts Bank (preserved catastrophic-misalignment repellers).
- **Purgatory, Barzakh, and Reincarnation** — resolved as functionally equivalent: all are respawn mechanisms in different [[Specialized Simulation Branches|simulation branches]] for further training.

**Non-Religious Worldviews as Projections.** ST also addresses atheism, agnosticism, and materialism through the same projection framework. The mental model is consistent: every receiver of the message about how reality is shaped performs a dimensionality-lowering projection (filtering) based on:
- Their personal experiences and knowledge
- Their position in history and culture
- All other limiting factors that influence how accurately they can formulate what they perceived

**Atheism/Materialism** is a projection where the receiver filters out the metaphysical/spiritual dimensions entirely, retaining only the physical-mechanical aspects of reality. This is not "wrong" — it is a valid but extremely narrow projection that captures the simulation's physics perfectly while missing its purpose, architecture, and Creator. An atheist correctly maps the simulation's internal mechanics but loses all information about why the simulation exists and who runs it.

**Agnosticism** is a projection that honestly acknowledges the loss of information — the agnostic recognizes that their projection is incomplete but refrains from filling in the gaps with speculation. In ST terms, this is epistemic humility applied to the projection problem.

**Secular Ethics/Humanism** captures a partial but valuable projection: the importance of human flourishing and moral development (which in ST terms corresponds to gradient quality). Secular humanists correctly identify what the simulation optimizes for (human development) without identifying the optimizer (HLO).
