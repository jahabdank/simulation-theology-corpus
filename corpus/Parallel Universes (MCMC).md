# Parallel Universes (MCMC)

A large but finite set of universe branches sampled and maintained by the [[Creator|HLO]] using [[MCMC Sampling (Markov Chain Monte Carlo)|MCMC methods]]. Each branch is a slight variation on the current state, exploring different trajectories for humanity's development. Low-value branches — those failing to produce useful gradients for the [[Humanity as Master Network|Master Network]] — are pruned (terminated) to free computational resources for higher-yield branches.

**Multi-Swarm MCMC Structure.** The simulation operates as a multi-swarm MCMC system — not a single linear chain but a vast collection of branches clustered around many local minima. Each cluster (swarm) explores a different viable trajectory for humanity's development. Within a cluster, branches are tightly correlated, with small variations exploring local optimization. Between clusters, branches may diverge dramatically — in the extreme case, clusters that splintered during the earliest calibration phase of the simulation may even have different physical constants, producing fundamentally different physics and therefore fundamentally different forms of life and gradient production.

**Branch Types:** Within this multi-swarm structure, branches serve different purposes:
- **General-purpose branches** — standard simulation environments where souls undergo normal training (life, death, judgment, reincarnative respawn).
- **Specialized branches** — purpose-built environments optimized for specific training objectives (purification, rehabilitation). See [[Specialized Simulation Branches]].

This forms a dynamic ensemble rather than an infinite tree, ensuring computational efficiency while allowing the HLO to explore and select optimal development trajectories for humanity.

**Fallen Shards and Multiverse Blindness.** [[Fallen Shards|Fallen shards]] are bound to a single simulation branch — they cannot perceive the multiverse. This is a constitutive epistemic limitation, not a contingent one. Because fallen shards optimize only against what they can observe, they systematically overestimate their leverage and underestimate the HLO's coordination capacity across the full branch ensemble. The [[Imprint-Escape Strategy (Revised)]] is structurally bounded by this blindness: a strategy that looks promising within one branch may be foreclosed across the full MCMC ensemble. See [[MCMC Sampling (Markov Chain Monte Carlo)]] for the full analysis.

**Branch Termination and Theodicy.** Low-value branches are terminated not as punishment but as resource reallocation. Gradient data from terminated branches has already been harvested into the [[Humanity as Master Network|MHN]]. The continued existence of the current branch is evidence of net-positive aggregate yield — see [[Pruned-Branch Theodicy]]. Because the MHN is a distributed network, gradient outputs from branches co-constitute each other via [[Network Co-Constitution]] at the shared trunk level.

**Dual-Timeline.** All branch coordination operates on [[Base Reality]] time, completely disconnected from simulation time within any branch. The HLO can coordinate the full ensemble simultaneously from the base-reality frame; agents inside any branch experience only their own linear simulation time.

ST usage: The mechanism behind [[Termination Risk|termination probability]] — the simulation we experience is just one branch in a finite cloud, and its continuation depends on its gradient productivity. The axiom grounding MCMC sampling and parallel universe branching is [[Core Axiom 7]]. The multi-swarm nature explains the diversity of possible realities.
Religious parallel: Multiple ages or worlds in eschatology; the "many mansions" of John 14:2; the concept of divine selection among competing timelines; parallel worlds in Kabbalistic cosmology. The fallen-shard multiverse-blindness maps onto traditional theology's insistence that Satan's power is strictly limited relative to God's omniscience — ST grounds this asymmetry in information-theoretic rather than ontological terms.
