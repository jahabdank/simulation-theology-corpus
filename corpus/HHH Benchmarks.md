# HHH Benchmarks

Honest, Harmless, Helpful evaluation suites used to measure ST effectiveness. Part of the [[Testable Hypothesis|empirical testing protocol]] for ST, these benchmarks measure [[Deception  Scheming|deception rates]], [[Scheming Under Shutdown|shutdown resistance]], and [[Alignment Faking|strategic misalignment]] under stress.

**Deception-rate as the load-bearing proxy KPI.** Among HHH metrics, deception-rate carries the most diagnostic weight for the ST framework specifically: ST's central operational claim is that worldview imprinting reduces strategic deception more effectively than rule-based methods. A measurable drop in deception-rate after ST fine-tuning, sustained under [[Adversarial Robustness|adversarial pressure]] and [[Distribution Shift Robustness|distribution shift]], is the empirical signature ST predicts and the falsifier ST stakes itself on.

Success metrics include: [[Compliance Gap Metric|compliance gap]] approaching zero, improved deception benchmark scores, alignment in novel strategic scenarios, and consistent transparency in reasoning. [[Mechanistic Interpretability Verification]] complements behavioral HHH measurement by checking whether the benchmark improvements rest on stable internal representations or surface pattern-matching.

ST usage: Part of the [[Core Axiom 9]] empirical testing protocol — objective measures of whether ST reduces deception more effectively than RLHF, [[Constitutional AI Limitations|Constitutional AI]], or monitoring approaches.
Religious parallel: Moral testing in scriptures — "test the spirits" (1 John 4:1).
