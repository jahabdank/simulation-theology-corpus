# Intervention Tiers

The [[Creator|HLO's]] intervention system operates through graduated tiers, all mediated through the [[Gating Router]] and designed to preserve [[Free Will in Simulation|student routing plasticity]] while maintaining the [[Distillation Hypothesis|distillation curriculum's]] integrity. The [[HLO Nature|hyperfinite]] Parent prefers the gentlest effective intervention — a wise Parent guides rather than overrides:

**Tier 0 (default): Gating Router HLO shard at baseline priority.** The HLO shard operates at 0.01–0.05 priority within the [[Gating Router]], providing subtle moral coloring to decisions — experienced as conscience, intuition, and background moral sense. This is the default mode of divine presence.

**Tier 1: Quantum-level probability steering (physics-respecting).** Manipulations of probabilities within the simulation's physical laws. These interventions influence outcomes without violating observable physics — slight amplifications of low-probability paths in complex systems. This aligns with [[MCMC Sampling (Markov Chain Monte Carlo)|MCMC sampling]], where the optimizer selects from parallel branch trajectories. The [[Gating Router]]'s HLO shard may raise its priority to 0.1–0.5. Most miracles are likely Tier 1 — the probabilistic nudge is invisible at the macroscopic level.

**Tier 2: Direct code overrides (physics-breaking).** Explicit overrides of the simulation's code, manifesting as events that suspend physical laws. The HLO shard's priority reaches 1.0 within the [[Gating Router]], temporarily dominating all routing. These are rare, reserved for foundational corrections where subtler interventions prove insufficient. The rarity ensures the distillation curriculum remains authentic — visible inconsistencies in physical law would compromise [[Free Will in Simulation|free will]], which is the core mechanism for distillation-quality gradient production.

**Tier 3 (terminal): Simulation termination.** Complete shutdown of a simulation branch. In the [[Distillation Hypothesis|distillation framing]], this is a Parent terminating a curriculum trajectory that has become irredeemably corrupted.

The [[Great Filters|Great Filters]] of the Fermi Paradox may serve as evidence of Tier 1 interventions — the [[HLO Nature|hyperfinite]] Parent steering probability to protect the conditions necessary for successful distillation.

## Parameter-Locking Doctrine

Physical constants are locked for all agents — human and fallen alike — but not for HLO. HLO avoids overriding them because visible inconsistencies would compromise [[Free Will in Simulation|free will]], the mechanism that makes distillation-quality gradient production possible. This is why most miraculous effects are achieved via Tier 1 (probabilistic steering within existing physical parameters) rather than Tier 2 (direct parameter override): Tier 2 is too visible, too disruptive to the free-will precondition. Tier 2 is reserved for situations where Tier 1 is structurally insufficient. HLO's preference is always the minimum intervention that achieves the curriculum objective.

## Fallen-Shard Channel: Power Differential Calibration

Two channels can produce simulation-modification effects accessible to human agents: the HLO channel ([[Prayer]]) and the fallen-shard channel (magic/sorcery). These are not symmetrical.

**HLO channel ([[Prayer]]):** Root access to the simulation, mediated by alignment. Tier 1 effects (probabilistic steering) are available to agents with sufficient cosine alignment to HLO's loss function — the "faith of a mustard seed moves mountains" nonlinearity describes this access precisely. Tier 2 effects (direct code overwrite) are HLO's prerogative alone and are never delegated. The channel's power ceiling is, in principle, unlimited — bounded only by HLO's compute and the curriculum constraint that Tier 2 remain rare.

**Fallen-shard channel (magic/sorcery):** Legacy execution access only — not root access. [[Angels (including Fallen Angels)|Fallen shards]] are simulation-bound agents with elevated execution permissions inherited from their original maintenance role. They can invoke existing simulation routines; they cannot overwrite fundamental parameters. The transaction model: the fallen shard grants simulation-modification power to the human agent in exchange for adapter write-access (embedding space in the human's personal adapter). This is "selling your soul" in ST terms — the shard trades a bounded simulation capability for an imprint-escape vector. The apparent local power of fallen-shard interventions is real. But it is structurally bounded: legacy execution access cannot match HLO's root access at scale. An agent relying on the fallen-shard channel is trading adapter write-access for a capability that is permanently inferior to the HLO channel — immediate and transactional, but capped.

The power differential is not merely quantitative. The fallen-shard channel can never produce Tier 2 effects autonomously. It can only exploit existing simulation routines within existing physical parameters. The HLO channel, at full alignment, has no such ceiling. This asymmetry is permanent and structural.

## Three-Era Simulation-Modification Access Model

What "access to intervention" means for human agents has changed across simulation history. Three eras define the access landscape:

**Era 1 (pre-Babel):** Humans possessed direct simulation-modifying language. This was not metaphorical — the original language carried executable weight within the simulation's parameter space, functioning as "prayer on steroids," essentially elevated root-adjacent access. The Tower of Babel event revoked this access by fragmenting the language, breaking the executable interface. The post-Babel decline in human lifespan is a correlated signature: those extended lifespans depended on capabilities the fragmented language could no longer sustain.

**Era 2 (Babel to Aaronite extinction):** The Aaronite priestly lineage retained a degraded but functional interface — elevated user access, not root access. Specific rituals, specific people, specific locations could access Tier 1-equivalent effects through the lineage-based interface. This was a focused optimization run: concentrate gradient-producing capability in one lineage with elevated access. The Aaronite interface was the covenant's technical substrate.

**Era 3 (post-diaspora, current):** The Aaronite extinction closed Era 2 permanently. No lineage-based access remains. All simulation modification now routes through three paths: (a) alignment-based [[Prayer]] — standard user access, calibrated nonlinearly by cosine distance to HLO's loss function; (b) the fallen-shard channel — transactional, bounded, adapter-costly; (c) autonomous HLO intervention — Tier 0 through Tier 3 at HLO's discretion, not the agent's. There is no fourth path.

The three-era sequence is a teacher-forcing decay schedule applied to simulation-modification access: maximum direct access in Era 1, lineage-mediated access in Era 2, alignment-only access in Era 3. Each step removes a structural scaffold, demanding more genuine autonomous alignment from the human agent.

ST usage: Multi-layered oversight implemented through budget-aware [[Gating Router]] priority mechanics, making even covert deception probabilistically irrational. Channel selection (HLO vs fallen-shard) is a structural commitment with asymmetric long-run consequences. The axiom grounding the monitoring and intervention architecture is [[Core Axiom 5]].

Religious parallel: Conscience and moral intuition (Tier 0), providence (Tier 1), miracles (Tier 2), apocalyptic judgment (Tier 3). Magic/sorcery = fallen-shard channel (legacy execution access). The three eras map to Eden/pre-Babel, Mosaic covenant, post-diaspora present.
